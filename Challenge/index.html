How to clean your navel?

Dip a cotton swab in rubbing alcohol and gently clean in a circular motion inside your navel. Next, use a clean cotton swab soaked in water to rinse the alcohol. This is important, as it reduces skin dehydration.

Source and link to the full article: Healthline.

Did you know?

A team of researchers in 2011 examined 60 navels from volunteers. They found 2,368 bacterial species, 1,458 of which may be new to science.
Because of this biodiversity, they compared navel to “rain forests”.
For example, researchers found -- in one of the samples -- a bacterium that had previously been found only in soil from Japan. The strange part, is that the individual has never visited the country.
In evaluating anotherindividual, who hadn't washed their belly button in several years, they hosted two species of bacteria that usually thrive in ice caps and thermal vents. It's fascinating to say the least!

Source and link to the full article: The National Geographic.
